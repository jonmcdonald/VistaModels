Connectivity in Block Diagrams

Signals, channels and binders provide the connectivity between instances in a block diagram. Every component instance has sockets or ports that represent its external connections.

  • Binders in SystemC do not carry information from one point to another in a design; they are simply used to represent connectivity between pins (sockets) of components: these are used in cases where a direct connection is required, without the need for an intermediate signal/channel.
  • Signals and channels, in contrast, carry information from one point to another in a design. In Vista, as in SystemC, the information carried by signals/channels is typed. This means that, in addition to a name, every signal/channel is characterized by its type.
