Graphically Manipulating Channels

Once you have drawn a channel, you can adjust its shape or move the entire channel (shape unchanged) from place to place. If you do the latter, the channel retains its shape and any intermediate nodes, but its startpoint and endpoint do not necessarily remain connected as before.

Selecting a channel — Nodes and segments. When two lines representing channels intersect, a node is created. A segment is any part of a line that begins either at the start of a channel or at a node, and that extends to the next closest node (if there is one) or to the end of the channel. If there are no nodes on a signal, the entire channel is a single segment. When you select a channel, only the segment up to the closest node is selected. To select a signal from endpoint to endpoint, double-click it.
